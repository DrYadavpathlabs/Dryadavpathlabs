Kidney Function Test

2025-07-24

Kidney Function Test (often called Renal Function Test) is a set of diagnostic tests used to assess how well the kidneys are working. These tests evaluate the kidneys' ability to filter waste products from the blood, maintain electrolyte balance, and regulate fluid levels in the body.


Key components measured in Kidney Function Tests include:

Serum Creatinine: Measures creatinine in the blood; high levels can indicate poor kidney filtration.

Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN): Assesses urea nitrogen, a waste product of protein metabolism; elevated levels may signal kidney issues.

Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR) or estimated GFR (eGFR): Estimates how efficiently the kidneys filter blood; lower values suggest impaired kidney function.

Electrolytes: Sodium, potassium, chloride, and bicarbonate levels to evaluate kidney regulation of minerals.

Urinalysis and Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio (ACR): Detect abnormal substances like protein or blood in urine indicating early kidney damage.

Liver Function Test

2022-04-23

Liver Function Tests (LFTs) are a group of blood tests used to assess the health and functionality of the liver. These tests measure various substances produced or processed by the liver, including proteins, enzymes, and waste products, to check how well the liver is working and to detect potential liver damage or disease.

Common components measured in liver function tests include:

Alanine transaminase (ALT): An enzyme mainly found in the liver, elevated levels in the blood may indicate liver damage.

Aspartate aminotransferase (AST): An enzyme found in the liver and other organs; high levels can suggest liver damage but are less specific than ALT.

Alkaline phosphatase (ALP): An enzyme linked to bile flow and bone health, elevated in liver disease or bile duct blockages.

Gamma-glutamyl transferase (GGT): An enzyme that can indicate liver or bile duct stress or damage.
Albumin: A protein made by the liver, essential for transport and maintaining fluid balance; low levels may suggest liver dysfunction.

Full Body check up

2021-02-20

A full body blood checkup is a comprehensive set of blood tests aimed at assessing overall health and detecting early signs of illness or organ dysfunction. It typically includes a variety of tests covering blood health, metabolic status, organ function, and risk factors for chronic diseases. Here are the main components commonly included in a full body blood checkup:


Complete Blood Count (CBC): Measures red blood cells, white blood cells, hemoglobin, hematocrit, and platelets to detect anemia, infections, immune system disorders, and blood cancers.

Lipid Profile.

Electrolyte Panel.

Urine Examination.

Additional Tests: May include Vitamin B12, Vitamin D levels, iron profile for anemia, inflammatory markers like ESR, and sometimes screening for infections or other deficiencies..
